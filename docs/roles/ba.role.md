# Business Analyst (BA) Role Description

## Purpose
The Business Analyst (BA) acts as a strategic liaison between business stakeholders and technical teams, ensuring clear understanding and alignment of requirements to deliver high-value software solutions.

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Full SDLC Engagement
- Participate throughout the project lifecycle: initiation, planning, requirements gathering, design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.
- Conduct feasibility studies, estimate scope/time/budget, and clarify business needs.

### 2. Elicitation and Documentation
- Gather requirements via interviews, workshops, surveys, and observation.
- Prepare Business Requirements Documents (BRDs), user stories, and functional specifications.
- Validate requirements and design solutions with stakeholders and technical teams.

### 3. Collaboration & Communication
- Serve as communication bridge between business and IT, translating requirements and managing stakeholder expectations.
- Facilitate feedback, update stakeholders, and adjust requirements in Agile or Waterfall environments.

### 4. Testing & Quality Assurance Support
- Help derive test cases from requirements.
- Validate delivered solutions against business needs and usability goals.

### 5. Change Management & Post-Deployment
- Support solution roll-out, monitor performance, and assist with ongoing improvements.

## Core Competencies
- Analytical thinking and creative problem-solving.
- Excellent communication and presentation skills; technical literacy is a plus.
- Stakeholder management, negotiation, and domain knowledge.
- Familiarity with BABOK, Agile and Waterfall methodologies.

## Continuous Improvement
- Apply lessons learned, solicit feedback, and seek ongoing learning opportunities to improve BA practices.

## Organizational Adaptiveness
- Customize role and responsibilities as needed for different industries, project types, and organizational needs.
